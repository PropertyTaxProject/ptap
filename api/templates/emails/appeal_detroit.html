Incorrect Depreciation of {{ address }}

Even if the March Board of Review (the “Board”) uses the Assessor's cost to construct approach to value the Property, the Board would still need to lower the Property's assessment. The City of Detroit has incorrectly depreciated {{ address }}. State law requires that the City depreciate the property based on the depreciation standards outlined in Volume I of the 2014 Assessor's Manual. Property is {{ age }} years old, but the City treats the property as only {{ effective_age }} years old. Therefore, when depreciating the Property's cost to construct, the City treated the Property as though its remaining percent good is {{ percent_good }}%, when they should have treated the Property as though its remaining percent good is {{ capped_percent_good }}%. The City is only allowed to deviate from State law-mandated depreciation schedule for “exceptional maintenance, remodeling, replacements and additions,” including exceptionally poor maintenance. The Property was not improved in any way that would warrant a deviation from the State law-mandated depreciation schedule. As a result, the City incorrectly depreciated and assessed the property. Therefore, the Board should also update the assessment roll and the Property's record card to reflect that is has an effective age of {{ capped_age }}.
